Problem framing

We don’t have a tooling problem.
We have a control-plane problem.

From a single team’s view, ADF + Databricks + Snowflake “works.” From an Optum Health view, we see something very different:

  • 5+ RA/Quality warehouses & lakehouses: ECDH, OCSP Facets, CDP, OHBI, HEDIS, Cozeva, DataCore, DataRAP…
  • 3+ operational warehouses and 4+ major data marts (Economics, RADV/RAF, Stars, Provider Incentives, Financials).
  • Dozens of inbound systems: PM, claims, encounters, labs, immunizations, eligibility, enrollment.
  • A zoo of tools: ADF, Databricks, Snowflake, dbt, Airflow, Airbyte, Great Expectations, Kafka, custom services.

Each team manages its own pipelines, metadata, lineage, and access model.

Without a unified data control plane, everything is “custom.” Slide 2 / 11
Today’s reality

Symptoms of a Fragmented Data Estate

  • Inconsistent metadata & lineage
    Different tools, different patterns; lineage lives in code or PowerPoint.
  • Scattered catalog & discovery
    No single RA/Quality catalog; multiple “truths” for member, provider, HCC, measures.
  • Access control silos
    Snowflake roles, Databricks UC, ADF security, Kafka ACLs – no unified PHI policy.
  • Duplicated logic
    Eligibility, enrollment, RAF logic re-implemented across warehouses and marts.
  • Audit & compliance pain
    For CMS / RADV / Stars questions, lineage is reconstructed by chasing teams and reading notebooks.
Net effect

Governance happens in spreadsheets and slideware, not in the platform. Risk is high, reuse is low, and time-to-trust is slow.

“We standardized tools for pipelines, but never standardized how we describe, govern, and share the data itself.”

Tools are standardized locally; governance is not standardized globally. Slide 3 / 11
Limitations at OH scale

Why “ADF + Databricks + Snowflake”
Isn’t Enough for Optum Health

That stack solves the problem: “Let team X move data from source Y into warehouse Z.”
It does not solve: “Unify RA, Quality, Finance, Ops & Provider data under shared governance.”

  • No single place for end-to-end RA/Quality lineage (payer → ECDH → marts → reports).
  • No consistent RA/Quality domain model across ECDH, CDP, OHBI, HEDIS, Cozeva, DataRAP.
  • No centralized PHI/PII classification and policy that spans all engines.
  • Hard to rationalize duplicated marts and pipelines; hard to retire legacy flows.
  • Hard to answer regulators’ “show me exactly how you produced this RAF score” questions.
We need a unified data control plane, not just more pipelines. Slide 4 / 11

Positioning

Fabric + Snowflake: Control Plane + Compute Plane

Fabric (Control Plane)
  • OneLake as the shared, governed data foundation.
  • Fabric Data Factory to orchestrate domain pipelines.
  • Lakehouse / Warehouse as RA/Quality domain stores (Bronze/Silver/Gold).
  • Purview for catalog, lineage, classifications, and policies.
  • Domain workspaces for RA, Quality, Finance, Ops, Providers.
Snowflake (Compute Plane)
  • High-performance analytic engine for curated Gold data.
  • Home for RA/RAF marts, Stars/Quality marts, Economics, Finance marts.
  • Consumes governed domain data from Fabric / OneLake.
  • Continues existing investment and skillset, but under a unified governance umbrella.

We are not replacing Snowflake. We are making Fabric the unified governance and data foundation that Snowflake (and others) plug into.

Fabric governs. Snowflake computes. Both are strategic. Slide 6 / 11
Risk & Quality example

Today’s RA / Quality Lineage Story

Example question: “Show CMS how this member’s RAF score was produced.”

  • Payer eligibility file lands in storage → ADF pipeline → Databricks transforms → loaded into ECDH.
  • From ECDH, custom jobs push slices into DataRAP, HEDIS provisioning, Cozeva, Stars, RAF/Economics marts, etc.
  • Each hop is implemented differently (dbt, notebooks, scripts), with its own “local” metadata.
  • Lineage is reconstructed manually by:
    • Chasing individual teams and SMEs.
    • Reading code, notebooks, and old diagrams.
    • Reconciling conflicting notions of “source of truth.”

This is slow, fragile, and risky for RA, RADV, and Stars audits.

Our most regulated processes depend on ad-hoc lineage reconstruction. Slide 7 / 11
Future state

RA / Quality with Fabric + Snowflake

  • Step 1 – Ingest to OneLake
    All inbound payer, clinical, PM data land in OneLake (Bronze), orchestrated via Fabric Data Factory.
  • Step 2 – Harmonize in RA/Quality domains
    RA/Quality logic lives in Fabric Lakehouse/Warehouse (Silver), with shared domain models.
  • Step 3 – Curate and publish
    Curated Gold data products are:
    • Exposed inside Fabric for cross-domain use, and
    • Published into Snowflake RA/RAF and Stars marts for analytics.
  • Step 4 – Govern with Purview
    Purview shows end-to-end lineage, classifications, and access policies from source files to marts to reports.
  • Step 5 – Audit-ready
    For a given member’s RAF score, we can open the lineage graph and show “source → transformations → controls → mart value.”

Regulators don’t care which ETL tool we used. They care that we can prove complete, governed lineage. Fabric + Purview gives us that.

Governance becomes an operational capability, not a manual exercise. Slide 8 / 11
Addressing concerns

Anticipating CDO Team Objections

  • “We already standardized on ADF + Databricks + Snowflake.”
    Fabric includes the next-gen Data Factory and OneLake. We standardize governance and data, not just pipelines.
  • “Fabric is just for Power BI.”
    Fabric is an end-to-end analytics platform: data movement, engineering, warehousing, real-time, and BI. Using it only for BI leaves governance value on the floor.
  • “This change is too big.”
    Proposal is phased:
    • Start with RA/Quality domains and new/modernized flows.
    • Register existing Snowflake/Databricks assets into the Fabric/Purview catalog.
    • Migrate strategic ADF pipelines into Fabric over time, not via big-bang.
  • “We’ll duplicate cost.”
    Fabric acts as the control plane; Snowflake and Databricks remain compute planes. We redirect spend from fragmented tooling into a governed, shared backbone.
The proposal is evolutionary, not revolutionary. Slide 9 / 11
Decision

What We’re Asking the CDO Team to Approve

  • 1. Recognize Fabric as the OH Unified Data Foundation
    Not just for Power BI – approve Fabric Data Factory, Lakehouse, and Warehouse for RA/Quality domains.
  • 2. Adopt Fabric + Purview as the standard governance plane

    Establish RA, Quality, Finance, Ops, Provider domain workspaces in Fabric mapped to ECDH, CDP, OHBI, HEDIS, Cozeva, DataRAP, and related marts.
  • 4. Treat Snowflake as a strategic Gold-layer warehouse
    Continue to use Snowflake heavily, but with upstream data and policies unified in Fabric / OneLake.
Approve Fabric as the governance fabric for Optum Health data. Slide 10 / 11
